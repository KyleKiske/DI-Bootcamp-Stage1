Michael Schumacher

Seven times World Drivers' Champion in Formula 1

Michael Schumacher (right) with his Ferrari team principal Jean Todt watching free practice session

This is the timeline of Michael Schumacher's career

  • 3 January 1969 - born in Hürth, North Rhine-Westphalia, West Germany

  • 1984 - Junior German Karting Champion

  • 1988 - made debut in single-seater racing, won Formula König series

  • 1990 - won German Formula Three Championship

    Schumacher driving his WTS Racing Formula Three car

  • 25 August 1991 - debut in Formula One as a replacement for the imprisoned Bertrand Gachot.

    Michael Schumacher driving Formula One car Jordan 191 through the first corner of Spa-Francorchamps

  • 8 September 1991 - debut for the Benetton-Ford team in Italian Grand Prix.

    Schumacher in 1991 Benetton-Ford driver apparel.

  • 1992 - first podium and first race win at Belgian Grand Prix. Finished third in the Drivers' Championship with 53 points.

    Schumacher driving Benetton B192 during Mocano GP

  • 1994 - won his first Drivers' Championship. Schumacher won six of the first seven races, was disqualified for two races and won championship by 1 point.

    Schumacher (in-front) driving Benetton B194 being chased by Damon Hill driving Williams-Renault during the last race in Adelaide

  • 1995 - Schumacher successfully defended his title with Benetton-Renault

    Schumacher celebrating his Win in Pacific GP and World Drivers' Championship defense

  • 1996 - Joined Ferrari, a team that had last won the Drivers' Championship in 1979. Lured Benetton employees Rory Byrne (designer) and Ross Brawn (technical director) to Ferrari. Finished Championship in third.

    Schumacher's first win with Ferrari at Spain GP

  • 1997- Lead a championship by the season finale, but was disqualified from the standind for causing a collision with main rival - Jacques Villeneuve.

    Schumacher (in red Ferrari) driving into Jacques Villeneuve during European GP

  • 1998 - Finished second in the Championship, edged out by Finnish Driver Mika Hakkinen.

    Furious Schumacher after crash with David Coulthard at Belgian GP.

  • 1999 - broke his leg at British Grand Prix after brake failure. Helped Ferrari win Constructors Championship with previous performance.

    Schumacher's crash in Silverstone

  • 2000 - Became three-time World Champion in a close battle with Hakkinen. Became first Ferrari champion since Jody Jody Scheckter in 1979.

    Michael celebrating championship victory with Jean Todt

  • 2001 - Became four-time World Champion.

    Schumacher at the podium after Hungarian GP

  • 2002 - Became five-time World Champion, equalling record of Argentinian Juan Manuel Fangio.

    Celebrating victory at German GP

  • 2003 - Became the first person to become Six-time World Champion.

    Schumacher at San Marino GP

  • 2004 - Became the first person to become Seven-time World Champion. His last champion title.

    Driving Ferrari F2004 at European GP

  • 2005 - Unsuccessful title defense. Finished third behind Fernando Alonso and Kimi Raikkonen.

    Only win from the season came at US GP due to Michelin supplied teams decision to withdraw from the race

  • 2006 - Retired at the end of the year finishing second in the Drivers' championship.

    Schumacher gets overtaken by main championship rival Fernando Alonso at penultimate race in Japan after suffering an engine failure

  • 2010 - Returned to F1 racing for Mercedes.

    Schumacher driving Mercedes at Malaysian GP

  • 2012 - Scored his last pole position and podium. Retired from sports once again.

    Schumacher at the podium in Valencia with Fernando Alonso and Kimi Raikkonen

  • 29 December 2013 - Skiing accident resulted in head trauma, due to which he fell into coma.

    Schumacher skiing

  • March 2014 - withdrawn from medical coma. Condition as of today is private to family members only.

I don't know of anyone who worked with Michael who had a bad word to say about him. Lots of people who raced against him had a different opinion but nobody I know who ever worked with Michael ever had a bad opinion about him because of his integrity, his commitment, his human side.

Ross Brawn - Ferrari Technical Director (1996 - 2006)
